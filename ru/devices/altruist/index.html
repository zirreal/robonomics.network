Altruist outdoor sensor

< All hardware

A smart sensor that collects environmental data - noise, dust, and temperature, and stores them on a decentralized sensor map.

Use case:

Become a provider of environmental data to your friends and neighbors in the area. Access truly decentralized measurements of air quality, noise, temperature, and other environmental conditions on the sensor map.

GET A DEVICEearly access for community members
Simple & hardcore ways to become a part of a sensor-based DePIN:

  1. Into the map

    Connect your device as simple as other smart devices and look at your environmental outdoor data on a decentralized fully distributed sensor map, this or any other independent fork.

    Open-source under the hood: check our firmware

  2. join the Connectivity

    Data processing is always a sensitive issue. That's why we offer a fully open-source connectivity module giving you the freedom to choose which servers to use for processing. No closed clouds, no opaque code.

    Robonomics Connectivity

    Robonomics established three Connectivity hubs to ensure a stable connection. This option switched on by default on the station.

    your own connectivity

    You may launch your own hub for receiving data from Altruist Civil Station and further processing.

    1. Deploy Connectivity
      • 2 CPU cores;
      • 4 GB RAM;
      • 40 GB storage;
      • Static IP.

      Than set up connectivity module and follow the instructions.

    2. Connect a station to your connectivity
      1. Open your sensor settings by its IP.
      2. Go to APIs settings.
      3. Check the checkbox for sending data to custom API and fill corresponding fields (server IP, ports, etc.).
    3. Join with your setup to global map (optional)

      If you want to publish data to the global map, you need to make two pull requests:

      1. Add IPFS ID of your Connectivity to agents list of sensor map: https://github.com/airalab/sensors.social/blob/master/src/agents.json
      2. Add parachain address of your Connectivity to template agents list of long-term storage server: https://github.com/airalab/RoSeMAN/blob/master/config/agents.template.json
    Open-source under the hood: check our connectivity module

  3. your own air quality map

    You have the opportunity to become the administrator of a sovereign sensor infrastructure, giving you full control over how data is collected, processed, and shared within your own independent network.

    1. Deploy a map
      1. Fork the map repository: https://github.com/airalab/sensors.social
      2. Edit the settings to customize your map, including the domain, provider, starting viewpoint, and your connectivity IPFS ID for the list of agents.
      3. Deploy your own version of the web interface and begin receiving real-time data from your sensor.

      Full guide here: https://github.com/airalab/sensors.social?tab=readme-ov-file#setup-your-own-map

    2. Launch long-term storage

      Simply collecting data from sensors is not enough; it needs to be processed. Tweak a server for collecting sensor’s data based on with open-source node.js software Robonomics Sensors Measure Analytics and Archive Node (RoSeMAN) and get your own working server for long-term map data storage.

      • 2 CPU cores;
      • 4 GB RAM;
      • 40 GB storage;
      • Static IP.
      1. Fork the RoSeMAN repository: https://github.com/airalab/RoSeMAN
      2. Choose Docker or Node.js (required MongoDB database) version.
      3. Add a parachain address of your Connectivity to agents list.
      4. Add RoSeMAN domain to remote provider of map.
      5. Run the project.
    Open-source under the hood: check our server-side software
